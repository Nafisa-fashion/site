Interview with Zareen

| Mar 6, 2016

Why Zhijaab?

Well, I just wanted an easy name as a pseudonym for my blog, and since it's called The Hijaab I felt that by just adding the first alphabet of my name, it would make it simple enough for ppl to remember me by.

Where are you from?

My father is Pakistani, and my mother is Filipino and I was born in Bahrain. When I was 7 years old, my family moved us to Dubai, UAE, and I’ve lived here ever since. So I’m a completely universal human being ! The perfect walking example for United Colors of Benetton :)

What are the roles/titles you hold in life?

I am a Beauty Entrepreneur. Makeup & Beauty are my absolute passion. I am a Self employed Makeup Artist, and have been for over 15 years and now I primarily focus on Bridals and Editorials. I also have my own online Makeup & Beauty store called Ezeenah.com that was launched short of 2 years ago. I am also a mother to a 2 year old toddler turning 13, called Ayaah.


Zareen with client Lilly Ghalichi from the show Shah’s of Sunset

Who's your role model and why?

My mom has always been my role model. She was practically a single mother of 2, and did everything in her power to give us everything that we needed. She was a revert to Islam and I learnt a lot about my Deen through her Sabr and perseverance through difficult times. My mom taught me the importance of being financially independent, and to never rely on anyone else to support me and that attitude has always pushed me to look outside the box. She was an extremely active woman and always on the go, and that attitude is what I’ve adopted into my life.

Favourite food>

Favorite food is a Yemeni dish called Hanidh. It’s basically lamb shoulders or ribs cooked in an oven for hours and served with biryani rice. The meat literally melts in your mouth. Absolutely finger licking good!

What is your favourite quote?

Usually from Oscar Wilde, Rumi and Imam Ali (AS). Here’s one of the many that I love “If you want to know where your heart is, see where your mind goes when it wanders.”

Why do you like Amaliah?

I love what Amaliah represents which is ‘choice’ ! There isn’t just one way to dress modestly. We can be modest and trendy, and it’s all about finding the right balance that can also complement your lifestyle. I think that it’s really important to have a platform where sisters can feel they can connect to and have their voices heard, and Amaliah does just that!

What does sisterhood mean to you?

Sisterhood means a lot of things to me. But the very first word I would use is empowerment. I believe true sisterhood is when you want to empower and enrich the lives of those around you, just as much as you would to be enriched and empowered. To want to help and nurture and help those around you to succeed, that to me is real sisterhood. To give those that are successful or struggling to make it, their rights of encouragement and support instead of critique and judgment, that to me is sisterhood.
