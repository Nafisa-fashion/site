Hello World.

By Selina & Nafisa | Dec 12, 2015

We thought it was about time we came out the closet and introduced ourselves.

Who are we? Why Amaliah? What is this?

It’s us. Team Amaliah. Two sisters. Selina (the big one) and Nafisa (the little one). There is a third one in between us but she’s busy being cool at school (she’s a teacher). We wrote this because we thought it was about time we came out the closet and introduced ourselves. To be honest we have been putting it off and almost hiding away because we didn't know what the reaction to Amaliah was going to be! But, Alhamdulilah we have been overwhelmed by the positive feedback and support. Thank you.

Nafisa

I’ll start (the little one). My name is Nafisa. I am a science and tech kid and I am passionate about impacting people. Whether it be giving someone a smile to brighten up their day or some positive words of encouragement to help someone along their journey. And that’s why Amaliah is here — to impact the Muslim community past just fashion. I set up Amaliah because it is time to bring not only modest fashion but also Muslim women to the forefront, in a positive way. Since starting Amaliah I have met bloggers who are doctors, lawyers, mothers, teachers, nurses, accountants, entrepreneurs and so much more. These are the Muslim women we all know, now it is time to come together and let the world see how amazing we are.

I love... eating, sleeping, travelling and Selina’s daughter (I’m the favourite aunt).

I want to... show people that regardless of your background, education or gender, you can achieve whatever it is you put your mind to.

Favourite quote..."It is personal self belief that changes the world." - A man I met in India.

Selina

I’m Selina the big one in more than one way*. I joined Nafisa in her passion by I guess being the sister she talked to about it and eventually we were working together on the movement. I'm passionate about empowering myself and taking the perspective of 'this is a test,' and when facing challenging situations seeing them as tests I can gain reward and learn from. You'll find me encouraging my friends and family to achieve their dreams whether personal or professional. *I'm 6 months pregnant today and have a daughter named Aishah, my love, my joy. One thing that drives me in life is to inspire her, I want her to know regardless of her circumstance, with Allah's guidance, anything is possible.

I love... the gift of guidance, Alhamdulilah.

I want to... be the best version of Selina each day and learn to make a better cup of tea!

Favourite quote..." God bless the woman who just wants to be better, do better, feel better; who refuses to give up despite the hell she experiences on earth. " Unknown
